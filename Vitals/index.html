The Five Vital Signs

Fundamental measurements that establish a patient's baseline health.

Temperature

~98.6°F oral

Core body heat. Varies by measurement site.

Pulse

60–100 bpm

Heart rate, felt as a pressure wave in an artery.

Respiration

14–18 /min

Breathing rate, rhythm, and character.

Blood Pressure

~120/80 mmHg

Force of blood on artery walls: systolic / diastolic.

O₂ Saturation

95–100%

% of hemoglobin carrying oxygen (SpO₂).

O.

Vital signs live in the Objective section of a SOAP note — they're measured, not reported. That's what makes them the reliable baseline the Assessment and Plan are built on. Subjective is what the patient says; vitals are what the numbers say.

Pulse & Heart Rate

Evaluated on three things: rate, rhythm, and volume. Drag the slider to explore the rate zones.

72bpm
Normal
4060100140
Normal (60–100 bpm). A resting adult heart rate in the typical range. Below 60 is bradycardia; above 100 is tachycardia. Both are types of arrhythmia.
Three characteristics
Rate. Beats per minute.
Rhythm. The regularity of the beats — steady or irregular.
Volume. The strength of the pulse: strong, weak, thready, or bounding.
Pulse deficit
Apical − Radial = Deficit

Subtract the radial pulse from the apical pulse. A gap means the heart is beating too quickly or too weakly for every beat to push a wave all the way to the wrist — a possible sign of a heart condition.

Blood Pressure

Two numbers, two phases of the heartbeat. Measured with two tools.

120

Systolic

Pressure in the arteries while the heart contracts and pushes blood out. Normal range ~100–120 mmHg.

80

Diastolic

Pressure on the artery walls while the left ventricle fills (relaxes) between beats.

Stethoscope — to hear the return of blood flow (Korotkoff sounds).
Sphygmomanometer — the BP cuff that applies and measures pressure.

Why BMI can give a false reading

  • It doesn't account for muscle mass — an athlete can read "overweight" while being very lean.
  • It ignores bone density and overall body composition.
  • It doesn't factor in racial and sex differences, so it can over- or under-estimate body fat.

BMI is a quick population screening tool — a starting point for a conversation, never a diagnosis or a measure of health on its own.
